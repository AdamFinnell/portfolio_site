ABOUT ME

“I usually describe myself as an artist who learned to code... Creativity has always been a big part of who I am—through drawing, music, and design—so when I discovered software development, it immediately clicked for me. I realized programming is really another form of creative expression. Instead of a canvas, you’re building tools and experiences that people can actually interact with. I’m originally from Kentucky, where my family still lives, and that keeps me pretty grounded and team-oriented. I enjoy building things that are both functional and engaging, and I tend to approach problems with a mix of technical logic and creative thinking.

I approach challenges with realistic expectations, which helps me stay resilient and focused on solutions. I’m outspoken in sharing ideas and providing constructive feedback, which strengthens the team’s problem-solving. I’ve encountered obstacles that challenged my plans, but I remained focused, learned from the experience, and worked on developing both my skills and discipline. I wasn’t discouraged by setbacks; instead, I focused on growing my technical skills and preparing myself for long-term success.

What really excites me about Banyan Labs is the combination of engineering excellence and social impact. The mission of creating opportunities for people who are motivated to grow really resonates with me. I’m excited about the chance to contribute to real projects, learn from experienced developers, and continue developing as a software engineer.”

Adam D. Finnell II


I developed my skills through the Persevere program, where I trained in full-stack JavaScript development. During that time, I worked with technologies like HTML, CSS, JavaScript, Node.js, Express, and MongoDB, and built several projects including REST APIs, web applications, and interactive front-end experiences. One example is a full-stack exercise tracker where users can log workouts and retrieve their training history. I’m also an ISSA-certified personal trainer, so I designed that project with real fitness workflows in mind.

Building projects like that helped me understand how the frontend, backend, and APIs all connect in a real application. I also became comfortable working with Git and GitHub for version control, branching, and collaborating on code. I love turning problems into opportunities to learn—and I’m always leveling up my code game, one line at a time.

Currently: Full-stack dev IRL. Mentally: An S-rank support character in a team of dreamers. Let’s build something epic together.
